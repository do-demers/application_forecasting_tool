Applications Forecasting Tool

The Public Service Commission (PSC) iis committed to ensuring that the Federal public service efficiently hires the workforce of the future that is capable and ready to deliver results for Canadians by helping departments and agencies decrease the Time-to-Staff (TTS). In order to do so, the PSC undertook a research project to identify the underlying key drivers of TTS.

The research findings indicate that the number of applications for a given selection process increases the TTS by approximately 0.15 days for each additional percent increase in applications. For example, for a selection process with 100 applications, it will take on average 1.5 days longer to staff if the process receives an extra 10 applications

Given these findings, predicting the number of applications, prior to advertising the position, would help HR professionals and hiring managers to effectively plan and organise their staffing activities in a more-informed way

Using lightweight open-source technologies and statistical analysis, the PSC has developed an innovative protoype called the application forecasting tool which provides estimates of the number of applications a process can expect, based on user-defined characteristics of the process

The tool also provides a list of all internal and external processes that closed between April 1st 2015 and March 31st 2018 that meet the characteristics selected by the user. Moreover, for each process in the list, the actual number of applications received and a link to the SOMC is also provided

This tool, currently in beta version, is made available for testing and user feedback (see feedback form). Future improvements will provide more robust estimates, including more key drivers of the number of applications and other user-experience features.
