Applications Forecasting Tool

The Public Service Commission (PSC) is committed to ensuring that the Federal public service efficiently hires the workforce of the future that is capable and ready to deliver results for Canadians by helping departments and agencies decrease the Time-to-Staff (TTS). In order to do so, the PSC undertook a research project to identify the underlying key drivers of TTS.

Research findings indicate that the number of applications, for a given job advertisement, increases the TTS by approximately 0.15 days for each additional percent increase in applications. For example, for a selection process with 100 applications, it will take on average 1.5 days longer to staff if the process receives 10 additional applications.

Given these findings, predicting the number of applications, prior to advertising the position, would help HR professionals and hiring managers to effectively plan and organize their staffing activities in a more-informed way.

Using lightweight open-source technologies and statistical analysis, the PSC has developed an innovative tool called the Application Forecasting Tool which provides the estimated number of applications per process, based on user-defined characteristics of the process.

The estimates shown represent the likely range for the average number of applications to a job advertisement with the characteristics selected by the user - it is not an estimate of the full range of possible outcomes. As such, given the actual number of applications might be outside the estimated values - results should be used for informational purposes only. For example, results can be affected by low numbers of job advertisements, or job classifications with diverse requirements.

The tool also provides a list of all internal and external job advertisements that closed between April 1, 2015 and March 31, 2019 that meet the characteristics selected by the user. Moreover, for each process listed, the actual number of applications received and a link to the job advertisement are also provided.

This tool was most recently updated on [DATE-to be added later] and include:

  • data from fiscal year 2018-2019
  • introduction of the academic level required in the essential qualification as a key driver
  • more precise options in the process type, number of positions and location variables

Inventories, alternates and cancelled job advertisements are excluded. Positions were counted once for each location and classification on the job advertisement. As such, totals derived from this tool might not reflect those published in the PSC's Annual Report.

The education field is derived from the education section of the essential qualifications of a ob advertisement. The information was gathered using an innovative text analysis approach that found words associated with education, (eg: law degree, two years of post-secondary education) and categorized them into the following 8 education levels:

  • Education not listed in essential qualifications
  • Some high school
  • High school diploma
  • Some post-secondary
  • Completion of post-secondary (including CÉGEP, College and Bachelor’s)
  • Professional degree (including Law degree and Medical degrees
  • Master’s degree
  • Ph.D. or higher degree
Where the essential qualification lists two possible levels of education, such as requiring a Bachelor’s OR a Master’s degree, the lower qualification is listed.

The process type field is derived from the “Who can apply” section at the beginning of a job advertisement. We used a Name-Entity Recognition (NER) technique to extract the organisations, locations and occupational groups listed in the area of selection. These entities correspond to the three types of eligibility criteria allowable for the area of selection, as outlined in the Public Service Employment Act. Using these tags, we then categorized the jobs into four process types:

  • External process: These are jobs open to the public.
  • Internal process, Open: These are jobs open to all public servants in Canada.
  • Internal process, NCR: These jobs are open to all public servants in the National Capital Region.
  • Internal process, Other: These jobs use one or more eligibility criteria in their area of selection.
For example: all employees of Parks Canada in the Western Region or all employees at level EX minus one.

Both of these categories were derived using innovative natural language processing algorithms to to turn unstructured text fields, like to “Who can apply” and the “education section” of job advertisements, into structured text. The main benefit of using such algorithms is to maximize information extraction to develop analysis and tools, such as the Application Forecasting Tool.

Application Forecasting Tool

Between April 1st 2015 and March 31st 2019, there were Z advertisement(s) that generated between XX and YY application(s). We estimate that for these characteristics, similar advertisements will receive between XX and YY applications.

Less than 10 observations, please use estimates with caution.
