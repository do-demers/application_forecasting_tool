Applications Forecasting Tool

The Public Service Commission (PSC)is committed to ensure that the public service efficiently hires the workforce of the future that is capable and ready to deliver results for Canadians help departments decrease the TTS. As such, the PSC undertook a research project to identify the underlying key drivers of TTS. The research findings highlighted that the number of applications increase the time-to-staff by approximately 0.15 days for each additional percent increase in applications. For example, for a selection process with 100 applications, it will take on average 1.5 days longer to staff if the process receives an extra 10 applications.

Given these findings, predicting the number of applications, prior to advertising the position, would help HR professionals and hiring managers’ plan and organise their staffing activities in a well-informed way.

Using lightweight open-source technologies and statistical analysis, the PSC developed an innovative product called the application forecasting tool. A tool that provides estimates on the number of applications a process can expect, based on user-defined characteristics of processes.

It also provides a list* of all processes that meet the characteristics selected by the user. For each process in the list, the number of applications received and a link to the SOMC is provided.
